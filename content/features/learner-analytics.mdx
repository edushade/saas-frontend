faq:
  title: "Learner Analytics FAQ"
  description: "Real questions, clean answers. Everything you'll wonder when you open the dashboard for the first time."
  items:
    - question: "What does the dashboard actually show?"
      answer: "Four KPI tiles at the top — total learners, active learners, average learning time and retention — each next to the previous period's number. Below that, an active-versus-inactive trend, a learner growth chart, a learning-time-by-day chart and a top-learners leaderboard. Everything you need to read the platform's health on a Monday morning."
    - question: "What's the difference between total learners and active learners?"
      answer: "Total learners is the full audience — everyone with a learner account on your platform. Active learners is the share that actually showed up in the period you're looking at. The gap between the two is your engagement story, and the trend chart makes it visible."
    - question: "How is retention calculated?"
      answer: "It's the share of new learners who come back to the platform after their first session — the people you didn't lose at the start. The number sits next to the previous period's number, so a small dip or a steady climb is right in front of you."
    - question: "Can I filter to a single cohort?"
      answer: "Yes. The cohort filter at the top of the page re-scopes every tile and every chart to that batch. So instead of a platform-wide story, you can read the cohort-specific one — useful for term-by-term comparisons or for stakeholders who care about one program."
    - question: "Can I compare two cohorts side by side?"
      answer: "Yes. Open the analytics for one cohort, then open another in a second tab. The numbers are comparable because the dashboard is built around comparison — same metrics, same time windows, same shapes — so you can see exactly what changed between intakes."
    - question: "Why is the learning-time-by-day chart useful?"
      answer: "It shows you the rhythm of your platform. Knowing your learners spend most time on Tuesday mornings means you can drop release notes, schedule live classes and time push notifications when people are most likely to be there — and stop sending Friday-night emails that nobody reads."
    - question: "Who's on the top-learners leaderboard?"
      answer: "The standout learners by score across the courses they're in — usually the people who finish quizzes early, score high and stay engaged. The card shows rank, name, score and course count, so you can spot champions to recognise, mentor or feature."
    - question: "Is the data anonymised?"
      answer: "The aggregate charts are aggregate — no single learner is identifiable in the trend lines or growth charts. The leaderboard names individuals because that's the point of recognition. Tenant isolation means an admin on one organisation never sees another organisation's data, even by accident."
    - question: "Can I export the data?"
      answer: "Yes. The dashboard has a CSV export, and every chart can be downloaded as data. Useful when you're prepping a board deck, sharing with a regulator, or piping the numbers into your own data warehouse."
    - question: "Does this work for both self-paced and cohort programs?"
      answer: "Yes. The metrics work the same way for either shape — the trend line traces engagement regardless of whether the program is on a schedule or open-ended. The dashboard handles a mix in the same view."
    - question: "Is the data live?"
      answer: "Close to live. Most charts refresh on a short cadence so the picture you see is current to within a few minutes. Real-time isn't always useful for analytics — a slight smoothing actually makes trends easier to read — but the lag is short enough that what you see now is what's actually happening."
    - question: "How is this different from Progress Tracking?"
      answer: "Progress Tracking answers \"how is each learner doing on this course?\" — granular, lesson by lesson. Learner Analytics answers \"how is the platform doing across all learners?\" — engagement, growth, retention, timing. Different questions, same source data."
    - question: "Who can see Learner Analytics?"
      answer: "Admins by default, plus instructors with the analytics-view permission. The view is gated by role, so a teaching assistant might see only their cohort's analytics while a platform admin sees the whole picture."
    - question: "Will this slow down at scale?"
      answer: "No. The analytics layer reads from indexed aggregates, not raw event tables, so the dashboard stays fast even when the platform has thousands of learners and millions of events behind it."
