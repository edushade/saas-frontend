faq:
  title: "Recorded Lessons FAQ"
  description: "How recorded lessons work in Edushade — formats, hosting, the in-platform player, progress tracking and resume."
  items:
    - question: "What counts as a recorded lesson?"
      answer: "Anything that is consumed asynchronously — video, audio, text, PDF or iframe. Each is a Lesson with its own lesson_type, sitting in the curriculum tree alongside live classes and quizzes. The platform ships a builder and a viewer for each of the five types."
    - question: "Which formats are supported?"
      answer: "Five today: video (self-hosted or direct URL), audio (podcasts and narrated content), text (rich-text editor with formatting), PDF (uploaded documents) and iframe (embed external content with progress tracking)."
    - question: "Can I self-host video and audio?"
      answer: "Yes. The lesson builder offers two options for video and audio: Self Hosted uploads the file to the platform's storage through the integrated file manager; Direct URL accepts any URL — your own CDN, an existing hosted file, or an HLS stream. Both paths play through the same in-platform player."
    - question: "Does the player support HLS streaming?"
      answer: "Yes. URLs ending in .m3u8 are detected automatically and routed through native HLS playback. Multi-quality streams work without configuration; the player picks the right rendition for the learner's connection."
    - question: "What about captions and chapters?"
      answer: "The Vidstack base supports captions and chapter tracks; configure them on your encoded source. Sprite thumbnails for the seek bar are wired up out of the box when a sprite URL is provided alongside the video."
    - question: "Can a learner resume where they left off?"
      answer: "Yes. The platform tracks the watch position locally (local storage, per-device, immediate) and remotely (learning_progress.last_position in seconds, on the server, cross-device). When the learner returns, the player seeks to the last known position automatically."
    - question: "Is progress tracked for every format?"
      answer: "Yes, with format-appropriate logic. Video and audio track last_position in seconds. PDF, text and iframe lessons track scroll/interaction-based progress. All five funnel into the same learning_progress table with status — not_started, in_progress, completed — and a time_spent counter in seconds."
    - question: "Can a lesson have attachments?"
      answer: "Yes. Each lesson carries an attachments list pulled from the file manager — slides, worksheets, datasets, transcripts. Attachments display alongside the main content on the learner page, with download enabled."
    - question: "Can I assign multiple instructors to a recorded lesson?"
      answer: "Yes. instructor_ids on the lesson is an array of platform users, so a guest expert can own one specific lesson without getting access to the rest of the course. The instructor list surfaces on the lesson page automatically."
    - question: "How does iframe progress work?"
      answer: "Iframe lessons accept an embed URL and load it inside the in-platform viewer. Progress tracking goes through useIframeProgress, which listens for postMessage events from the embedded content — so an interactive activity that posts a 'completed' event marks the lesson done. Otherwise, time-on-screen drives the progress percentage."
    - question: "Can recorded lessons be locked behind drip rules?"
      answer: "Yes. Recorded lessons are curriculum items, so all drip and prerequisite logic applies. Lock a video behind a quiz pass, release a PDF on a fixed date, or make a text recap available only after the live class is completed — the same engine that drives drip on live classes drives it on recordings."
    - question: "What player skins are available?"
      answer: "Two: Vidstack default (the platform's own skin) and Plyr (a Vidstack-Plyr layout). The skin is set per player instance, so a brand-aligned default can run alongside a Plyr-styled player on a different page if needed."
    - question: "Can lessons exist outside a course?"
      answer: "Lessons live inside courses through the curriculum builder. For standalone, sellable recorded content not tied to a curriculum, use Digital Products or Course Bundles to package and sell — and the lessons themselves stay inside their parent course."
    - question: "What does a learning_progress record look like for a recorded lesson?"
      answer: "It carries entity_type='lesson', the lesson UUID as entity_id, the user UUID, status (not_started / in_progress / completed), progress_percentage (decimal up to 100), time_spent in seconds, last_position in seconds (for video and audio), started_at and completed_at timestamps, last_accessed_at, and a JSONB metadata field for type-specific extras like watched-segments."
    - question: "Can I export progress data?"
      answer: "Yes. The learning_progress table is queryable per program, per lesson and per learner, with all timing, status and position fields included. Pull a section's worth of records to see who watched what, or a course's worth to feed analytics dashboards or accreditation reports."
