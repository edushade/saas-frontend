faq:
  title: "Student Panel FAQ"
  description: "How the learner experience is laid out — dashboard, curriculum, assignments, exams, calendar, noticeboard, messaging, payments and account control."
  items:
    - question: "What does the Student Panel actually contain?"
      answer: "Eight top-level sections in the sidebar — Overview, My Course, Assignment, Exam, My Calendar, Event, Notice Board and Notification — plus Payment History under its own group. Each opens a real route under /dashboard with its own UI, not a redirect to an admin screen. Profile and account control sit under the user menu."
    - question: "Where do learners find their courses?"
      answer: "Under My Course at /dashboard/enrollments. The page lists every enrollment the learner is on — courses, learning paths and cohorts — with progress, last-accessed timestamp and a Continue Learning button. Clicking through opens the curriculum runner, which loads the lesson, quiz or assignment they were on."
    - question: "How are lessons played?"
      answer: "The runner ships with seven lesson types rendered inline: video (HLS or MP4 with provider metadata), live (with meeting integration and password), audio, text (rich content), PDF (with viewer), embed (third-party iframes) and iframe (custom). Drip rules from the course are honoured — locked items show a lock state with the unlock condition."
    - question: "How are quizzes and exams taken?"
      answer: "The Exam tab lists every quiz the learner has open, with attempts, marks and time limits surfaced. The actual attempt runs at /dashboard/quiz-attempt under a clean layout — no sidebar, no clutter — with the optional security knobs (full-screen, copy-paste blocking, randomised order, webcam) honoured. Result pages link to a merit list when one is configured."
    - question: "How are assignments handled?"
      answer: "The Assignment tab lists every assignment due to the learner with deadline, attempts and status. Opening one shows the brief, accepts the submission — text, file upload or quiz, depending on the assignment type — tracks attempts, and links to a merit list when the cohort uses one. Instructor feedback flows back into the same view."
    - question: "What is on the personal dashboard?"
      answer: "A performance score, six stat tiles (ongoing courses, completed, time spent, live class attendance, quizzes completed, assignments completed) with progress rings and tooltips, upcoming classes, a streak with rewards, a my-courses card, a learning statistics chart, a leaderboard widget, remaining quizzes and assignments and an improvement areas card."
    - question: "Are streaks and leaderboards real or cosmetic?"
      answer: "Real. Streaks are computed from learner activity and tied to rewards visible on the dashboard. The leaderboard widget surfaces the cohort's standings and updates as quizzes and assignments are graded. Both are scoped per-tenant and per-cohort, so a learner only ranks against people in the same program."
    - question: "What does My Calendar show?"
      answer: "Live class times, assignment deadlines, exam windows, booked one-on-one sessions and event start times — every dated artifact the learner has skin in, in one calendar grid. The Events tab covers workshops and webinars specifically, so a learner can register, see joining details and find the recording afterwards."
    - question: "Notice Board vs Notifications — what is the difference?"
      answer: "Notice Board is the noticeboard surface — an editorial channel where the platform posts announcements with priority, scheduling, expiry and per-user read tracking. Notification is the transactional bell — assignment graded, certificate issued, payment successful and so on. Both have an unread badge in the sidebar; clearing one does not clear the other."
    - question: "Is messaging built in or do learners need a separate tool?"
      answer: "Built in. The /dashboard/message surface is a Discord-style space with direct messages between people, servers for cohorts or programs, channels inside servers for topic-based chat, and a forum sub-surface with topic threads. Cohort messaging works without a Slack or a Discord on the side."
    - question: "Can learners see what they have paid and request refunds?"
      answer: "Yes. Payment History at /dashboard/my-orders lists every order with status, items, totals and timestamps; clicking one opens the order detail and a downloadable receipt. The Refunds page handles refund requests directly — the learner files, the admin reviews, and the status flows back to the same screen."
    - question: "What account controls do learners get?"
      answer: "Edit profile, change email and phone (with OTP verification), rotate password, manage trusted devices, review the activity log, tune notification preferences per channel (email, SMS, in-app, push), and start an account deletion flow if they want out — all from /dashboard/profile/settings, no admin involvement required."
    - question: "Does it work on mobile?"
      answer: "Yes. The learner panel ships a bottom navigation bar on small screens, the sidebar collapses cleanly on tablet, and every page on /dashboard is laid out with a stack on mobile and a grid on desktop. The curriculum runner, quiz attempt screen and message surface are all designed for the smaller viewport too."
    - question: "Is the learner panel localized?"
      answer: "Yes. Every screen pulls translations from the active locale — sidebar labels, dashboard copy, stat tooltips, button text, the lot — and the platform's broader localization config (RTL, per-locale typography, region-aware date and number formatting) flows through the same way it does on the public site. A learner who switches locale gets the panel in their language without a reload artefact."
    - question: "How does the learner side stay in sync with the admin side?"
      answer: "Both sides are wired into the same backend services — enrollment, learning, billing, engagement, notification — so a course an admin publishes is immediately visible under My Course, an assignment graded shows up in the bell within seconds, and a refund decision lands on /dashboard/refunds without an export-import in between."
