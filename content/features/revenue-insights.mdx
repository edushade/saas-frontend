faq:
  title: "Revenue Insights FAQ"
  description: "What founders, operators and finance partners ask the first time they sit down with the numbers."
  items:
    - question: "What's on the dashboard when I open it?"
      answer: "Three headline tiles up top — total revenue with the change since last period, pending payments waiting to clear, and refund requests waiting to be processed. Below that, a daily revenue trend chart with the breakdown by program type, plus a distribution view and a top-programs leaderboard. Everything you need for the first read on the business in one page."
    - question: "How is total revenue calculated?"
      answer: "It's the sum of all completed payments in the period, in your tenant's reporting currency. The change tile shows you both the percentage and the dollar amount of the difference against the previous period of the same length. So if you're looking at this month, you're comparing it to last month, automatically."
    - question: "What time windows can I look at?"
      answer: "The revenue trend supports seven-day and thirty-day windows. The headline tiles compare to the matching previous period. The distribution and top-programs views work across whatever window the dashboard is showing. For longer ranges or custom windows, the CSV export is the path."
    - question: "How does the trend chart work?"
      answer: "The chart breaks revenue out by program type — courses, learning paths, cohorts, bundles, products, events, meeting plans — across the days in your window. Each program type is its own line with its own colour, so you see at a glance which one is climbing, which is flat, and which is dropping. Hover any day for the exact figures."
    - question: "Why does revenue split across so many program types?"
      answer: "Because Edushade supports them all. A platform might sell courses, learning paths, cohorts, bundles of programs, standalone products, live events and one-on-one meeting plans — and the dashboard treats each as a first-class revenue stream so the numbers reflect what's really going on, not a flattened total."
    - question: "What's the difference between the distribution and the top-programs table?"
      answer: "The distribution is the macro view — what share of your revenue comes from courses versus bundles versus events. The top-programs table is the detail — every course, path and cohort with the revenue it earned, sortable by amount. Use the distribution to understand the shape of the business; use the table to find the specific programs to talk about."
    - question: "How are cohorts handled in the top-programs view?"
      answer: "Each cohort gets its own row with its revenue, plus the parent course or learning path it belongs to. So you can see that the spring cohort of Algebra 101 earned X, sitting under the Algebra 101 course, without losing the context. Useful when a single course runs as multiple cohorts."
    - question: "What about pending payments?"
      answer: "Pending is the count and total of payments that have been initiated but haven't completed yet — usually waiting on the gateway to confirm or on a manual cash payment to be activated. The dashboard surfaces them so they don't disappear, and the platform auto-cancels stale pendings on a timeout to keep things clean."
    - question: "And refund requests?"
      answer: "Refunds appear as a count and total on the dashboard until they're processed. From the same admin you can review each refund request, approve or reject it, and the platform fires the right notification to the learner — initiated, completed or rejected — depending on the path. Total refunded is also tracked per payment so you can't accidentally overrefund."
    - question: "What does the pricing mix show?"
      answer: "How many of your entities are priced (courses, paths, cohorts, bundles, products, events, meeting plans), the average price across the catalogue, and which pricing model is most popular among them — one-time, subscription, installment or free — with the percentage. Useful for understanding whether your audience is on subscriptions or buying one-off."
    - question: "Are subscriptions and installments included?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade supports four pricing models — one-time, subscription, installment and free — and revenue from each flows into the same dashboard. The popular-model card tells you which way your audience is leaning; the per-program rows include subscription and installment revenue alongside one-time purchases."
    - question: "Is the data real-time?"
      answer: "Close to it. New payments update the totals within a minute or two. The trend chart refreshes on a short cadence. So when a big sale lands, you see it move on the dashboard. Daily aggregations smooth the noise so the trend lines stay readable, but the headline and tiles are current."
    - question: "Can I export the data?"
      answer: "Yes. The transactions list exports to CSV with full filters, the prices export covers the catalogue side, and individual revenue tables can be downloaded. Useful for finance reconciliation, board decks, regulator requests, or piping into your own warehouse."
    - question: "Is the data tenant-isolated?"
      answer: "Hard-isolated. Every payment, price and refund belongs to a single tenant; queries always filter by it; an admin on one organisation never sees another organisation's revenue, even by accident. The dashboard you see is yours alone."
    - question: "Who can see Revenue Insights?"
      answer: "Admins by default — specifically anyone with the price-view permission. Instructors don't see it unless you grant them the permission, which is the right default for most teams. If your organisation needs a finance role with read-only access, the Roles & Permissions builder handles that without giving them the rest of the admin."
    - question: "Will it scale on a busy platform?"
      answer: "Yes. The dashboard reads from indexed aggregates rather than raw transaction tables, so the page stays fast even when the platform processes thousands of orders a day. The trend chart, distribution and top-programs all paginate or sample as needed to keep the experience snappy."
