faq:
  title: "Educator Panel FAQ"
  description: "How educators sign in, what they see, what they can do, and how their experience shares the same shell as the admin."
  items:
    - question: "Where do educators sign in?"
      answer: "At /admin — the same URL the platform team uses. Edushade does not split educators off into a separate side app. The admin shell loads, the same dashboard renders, and the sidebar resolves to a teaching-scoped menu because the platform's RBAC gates every item by role and permission."
    - question: "Why is there no dedicated educator dashboard?"
      answer: "Because the admin dashboard already gives educators what they reach for — greeting, Quick Actions, System Metrics, course analytics, recent notices, enrollments and learning stats — and shipping a second dashboard would split the same data across two surfaces. The work that does belong only to learners (streaks, leaderboards tuned to one person, my-courses) lives on the learner panel at /dashboard, where it fits the audience."
    - question: "Which sidebar items do educators see?"
      answer: "Dashboard, Analytics (Course Analytics, Enrollment Trends, Course Performance), Resources (Learning Paths, Courses, Quizzes, Events, Categories, Tags, Bundles, Content Bank, Grading), Sales → Enrollments, Booking (Meeting Schedules, Meeting Plans), Communication (Discussion, Noticeboard, Notification), and Platform → People → All Users / Learners. Anything below that — Billing, Audit, Settings, Website, Instructors, Admins & Staff, Roles & Permissions — is admin-only."
    - question: "What program types can educators author?"
      answer: "All teaching-side ones: Courses with the curriculum tree, Learning Paths that bundle courses with prerequisites, Quizzes (practice, graded, survey, assessment) and Assignments (text, file upload, quiz). Cohorts attach to courses with start and end dates, weekly schedule and a max-seats cap. Each ships its own list view, edit screen and management tabs in the same admin shell."
    - question: "Which lesson types are available?"
      answer: "Seven: video (HLS or MP4 with provider metadata), live (with meeting integration and password), audio, text (rich content), PDF (with a viewer), embed (third-party iframes) and iframe (custom). The curriculum builder supports all seven side by side; learners get the right player on the runner without any switch."
    - question: "How is grading handled?"
      answer: "Through the Grading hub at /admin/settings/grading — programs, exam terms and a submissions queue across every program the educator owns. Quizzes auto-grade where they can; assignments and free-form questions land in the queue with the learner's submission, attempt count, attached files and instructor feedback fields ready to fill."
    - question: "Can educators see learners enrolled in their programs?"
      answer: "Yes. Sales → Enrollments lists every learner across every program the educator is on, with progress, status and bulk actions. Platform → People → Learners gives a tenant-wide learner table, also gated by the same permissions. The instructor cannot see admins, staff or other instructors — that part of People is admin-only."
    - question: "What about live sessions and bookings?"
      answer: "Booking → Meeting Plans creates the bookable templates — Calendly-style — and Meeting Schedules surfaces incoming bookings. Group, request-based and paid sessions all run on the same model. The OAuth integrations themselves (Google Calendar, Zoom) are connected by an admin under Settings, but every meeting an instructor accepts uses the same calendar sync and notification stack."
    - question: "What can educators do under Communication?"
      answer: "Three things. Discussion lets them post and reply on threaded discussions tied to courses, paths, quizzes, events or cohorts. Noticeboard lets them publish announcements with priority, expiry, scheduling, pinning and read tracking — scoped to audiences they own. Notification covers transactional alerts; templates that ship with the platform fire automatically, and instructors with notification.send can trigger their own."
    - question: "Which analytics do educators get?"
      answer: "The same charts the admin sees, scoped to their programs. Course Analytics (per-course breakdown), Enrollment Trends (cohort growth) and Course Performance (completion + grade distribution) live under Analytics. The dashboard's course analytics chart and learning stats widget run on the same data, so a glance at /admin/dashboard already covers the headline metrics."
    - question: "Can multiple educators share a course?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade supports instructor, co-instructor and assistant roles per course, per cohort, per learning path or per individual lesson. A guest expert can own one lesson in a cohort without getting access to the rest. Permissions stay scoped to where the role is assigned, and the People views surface every educator on a program."
    - question: "What about reviews and feedback?"
      answer: "Reviews fire to the instructor automatically — the review.received notification template ships with email, SMS, in-app and push variants — and the same admin Reviews tab on a course or learning path lets the instructor respond, archive or mark a review for the moderation queue. The course-level rating-enabled toggle is theirs to flip on or off per program."
    - question: "What can educators NOT do?"
      answer: "Billing (pricing, EMI, scholarships, transactions, refunds, subscriptions, offers), Audit (audit and activity logs), Settings (Control Panel, Holidays, Integrations, Payment Gateways), the Website builder, the Instructors and Admins & Staff views under People, and the Roles & Permissions builder. All of those live behind the admin role and admin-only permissions, so an instructor's menu hides them entirely."
    - question: "Is the experience localized?"
      answer: "Yes. The admin shell is fully localized — sidebar labels, page headers, table columns, button text — and pulls from the active locale. Content owned by the educator (course title, description, lesson body, notice text, FAQ items) uses the LocalizedField shape, so a translation flow is the same as on the public site."
    - question: "What if my organization wants a different educator scope?"
      answer: "Use Roles & Permissions on the admin side to create a custom role with whatever permissions you need — teaching assistant with grading-only, content reviewer with read-only access, lead instructor with everything plus billing visibility. The sidebar reads from the same RBAC engine, so a new role gets a new menu without a code change."
