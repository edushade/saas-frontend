faq:
  title: "Announcements FAQ"
  description: "How announcements work in Edushade — audience targeting, priority, scheduling, expiry, pinning and read tracking."
  items:
    - question: "What is an announcement in Edushade?"
      answer: "A record on the notices table — title, rich-text body, audience type, audience IDs, priority, status, optional published_at and expires_at, plus pinned and recurring flags. The admin creates them under Communication; learners see them on a dedicated noticeboard with an unread badge in the sidebar."
    - question: "Who can I target with a notice?"
      answer: "Eight audiences: everyone on the platform (the empty audience type), a specific role, a course, a cohort, a learning path, an event, a quiz, or a hand-picked list of users. Audience IDs are stored as an array, so a single notice can target multiple cohorts or courses at once."
    - question: "How do priority levels work?"
      answer: "Four levels — low, normal, high and urgent — each with its own icon and colour on the learner panel. Priority does not change targeting; it changes how the notice presents, so a routine update reads differently from a deadline reminder."
    - question: "Can I pin a notice?"
      answer: "Yes. Toggle the pinned flag on the notice and it stays at the top of the noticeboard regardless of how many newer notices land underneath. Useful for ongoing reminders, semester-long policies and welcome notes."
    - question: "Can I schedule a notice for later?"
      answer: "Yes. Set status to scheduled and pick a published_at date and time. The notice stays out of the learner noticeboard until that moment, then flips to published on its own. Same flow for batch announcements timed to a cohort start."
    - question: "What about an expiry date?"
      answer: "Each notice carries an optional expires_at. Once the expiry passes, the notice drops off the active board automatically — no manual cleanup. The record itself is preserved for audit, and you can still surface it through the archive view."
    - question: "Can a notice attach files?"
      answer: "Yes. Notices accept any number of attachments through the platform's file manager — PDFs, images, schedules, docs. Attachments display alongside the body on the learner detail view, with download enabled."
    - question: "How is read tracking handled?"
      answer: "A read_tracking flag on the notice opts the message in. When a learner opens it, Edushade writes a notice_reads record with notice_id, user_id and read_at. The learner sidebar shows an unread count badge based on these records, capped at 9+."
    - question: "Can a learner mark everything as read at once?"
      answer: "Yes. The noticeboard sheet has a mark-all-as-read action that writes notice_reads for every unread notice in one call. The unread badge clears immediately."
    - question: "What states does a notice go through?"
      answer: "Four: draft (admin-only, in progress), scheduled (waiting on a published_at), published (live for the chosen audience), and archived (retired, kept for the record). The record itself is soft-deleted on removal so audit history is preserved."
    - question: "Are notice events triggered as notifications too?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade ships an announcement.published notification template — email, SMS and push variants — that fires when a notice is published in a course context, so learners receive an email with the title, preview and a read-more link in addition to seeing the notice on the noticeboard."
    - question: "What does the recurring flag do?"
      answer: "It marks a notice as repeating — useful for yearly reminders, semester-start messages or seasonal policies. The flag carries on the record; how it surfaces depends on the consuming view, but the data shape supports recurrence at the model level."
    - question: "Can I export notices?"
      answer: "Yes. Notices export to CSV with full metadata — title, body, audience type, audience IDs, priority, status, published-at, expires-at, pinned, recurring, read-tracking, thumbnail and audit fields — so a campaign of notices can be backed up, audited or moved between environments."
    - question: "How is a notice different from an in-course announcement?"
      answer: "They are the same record. A 'notice with audience type = course' is the in-course announcement. The course detail page surfaces those filtered to its own ID; the noticeboard surfaces every notice the learner is eligible for. One model, two views."
    - question: "Can multiple admins post notices?"
      answer: "Yes. Each notice carries a created_by and updated_by attribution, so a coordinator, an instructor and a registrar can all post on the same platform with clear authorship on every record."
