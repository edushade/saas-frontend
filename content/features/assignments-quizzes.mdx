faq:
  title: "Assignments FAQ"
  description: "How assignments work in Edushade — submissions, grading, deadlines, attempts and where they sit in your courses and cohorts."
  items:
    - question: "What is an assignment in Edushade?"
      answer: "An assignment is a piece of open-ended work — written, uploaded, or both — with a start time, an end time and a marks total. It is graded manually by an instructor or assistant, and the grade carries a score, learner-visible feedback and optional private comments."
    - question: "How is an assignment different from a quiz or assessment?"
      answer: "Quizzes and assessments are objective: they ask multiple choice, true-or-false, fill-in-the-blank style questions and grade themselves. Assignments are subjective: a learner produces something — an essay, a file, a project — and a person reads it. Both live in the same curriculum tree, but the workflow is different."
    - question: "What can a learner submit?"
      answer: "Two things, side by side: a rich-text response and any number of file uploads. The text and the files land on the same submission record, so grading happens in one view rather than across tabs."
    - question: "Can I attach files to the assignment brief?"
      answer: "Yes. Use the platform's file manager to add briefs, datasets, templates or rubrics. Edushade stores those attachments on the assignment itself, separate from learner uploads, so the brief and the answer never get confused."
    - question: "How are deadlines enforced?"
      answer: "Every assignment has a required start_at and end_at. Edushade validates that end is after start, shows learners a countdown until the window opens and another until it closes, and applies a missed state when the window closes without a submission."
    - question: "How are marks set?"
      answer: "Two fields: total_marks and passing_marks. Total has to be at least 1; passing cannot exceed total. The score on each submission is stored as a decimal with up to two places, so partial credit is precise."
    - question: "Who can grade submissions?"
      answer: "Anyone with the right role on the program — instructor, co-instructor or assistant. The grader's user ID is stamped onto the submission, and the graded-at timestamp is captured automatically."
    - question: "What status can a submission be in?"
      answer: "Three states: submitted (the learner has turned it in), graded (a grader has scored and posted feedback), and returned (the grader sent it back for another pass). Returning a submission keeps the previous attempt and adds a new one with an incremented attempt number."
    - question: "What is the difference between feedback and comments?"
      answer: "Feedback is shown to the learner alongside the grade — it is the explanation of why they scored what they scored. Comments are private to the team and useful for grading notes, calibration, or context the learner does not need to see."
    - question: "Can a learner resubmit?"
      answer: "If a submission is returned, yes — the learner sees the feedback inline and submits again, which creates a new submission record with the next attempt number. This keeps the full history of what changed across revisions."
    - question: "What about the merit list?"
      answer: "Two opt-in switches per assignment: show_merit_list_to_learner shows the learner their own ranking, and show_other_merit_list opens that ranking to everyone who submitted. Both are off by default — turn them on when ranking adds something to the experience."
    - question: "Where do assignments live in the curriculum?"
      answer: "Inside a course's section tree, alongside lessons, quizzes and downloadable files. Submissions are stamped with the program type — course or cohort — and the program ID, so the same assignment can be reused across cohort runs while keeping the records separable."
    - question: "Can drip rules apply to assignments?"
      answer: "Yes. Drip schedules target sections, lessons, quizzes, assignments and files, so an assignment can be locked until a prerequisite item is completed, scheduled to a fixed date, or held until a number of days after enrollment. The same drip engine that controls lessons controls assignments."
    - question: "Can I export assignment data?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade ships a CSV export of every assignment's full record — title, instructions, status, dates, attempts, time limit, marks, merit-list settings and order index — so you can back up, audit or move records into another system."
    - question: "What states can an assignment itself be in?"
      answer: "Draft, published or archived. Draft assignments are visible to admins only; published assignments open up at the start time; archived assignments stay in the data for reference but are removed from active curricula."
