faq:
  title: "Instructor Management FAQ"
  description: "How instructors are stored, assigned, surfaced and notified inside Edushade."
  items:
    - question: "How does Edushade represent an instructor?"
      answer: "As a row on the instructors table with a composite primary key — tenant_id, entity_type, entity_id, instructor_id. The same user can hold one instructor record per program they teach, with a role (instructor / co-instructor / assistant), an is_featured flag and the usual created_at / updated_at / deleted_at audit fields. Soft delete keeps the history when someone moves off a course."
    - question: "Can a single course carry multiple instructors?"
      answer: "Yes. The composite key is built for it. A course can have one instructor, three co-instructors and two assistants — each as their own row, each with their own role and featured state. The admin UI surfaces all of them on the course page, and the public instructor section renders them in the right order with the right credit."
    - question: "What roles ship out of the box?"
      answer: "Three: instructor, co-instructor and assistant. Instructor is the lead. Co-instructor shares ownership and shows up on the course page. Assistant has a teaching-assistant scope — visible to learners as support staff, with the same permission scope as instructors. Custom variations live on top of the platform's RBAC system if you need them."
    - question: "Where can I assign an instructor?"
      answer: "Four scopes: course, cohort, learning path and individual lesson. Lesson-level is the most precise — a guest expert can own one specific lesson without getting access to the rest of the course. The same instructors panel surfaces in every program type, so the workflow is identical wherever you assign."
    - question: "What is the featured-instructor flag for?"
      answer: "It pins one instructor as the headline face on the public course page. Useful when a course is taught by a team but marketed around a lead. Toggle it on the assignment row — no other instructor is removed, the visual order on the public page just respects the featured flag first."
    - question: "Where does the public instructor profile come from?"
      answer: "From the linked user record. Avatar, name, bio, professional summary, designation, expertise tags and six social links — LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, personal website — all live on the user. The public site reads from there, and a public stats endpoint exposes the instructor's teaching footprint so a profile page can show the courses, paths and cohorts they are on."
    - question: "Which notifications does an instructor receive?"
      answer: "Course-event notifications on every channel they have on: course published, unpublished, approved, rejected, submitted for review, new content added. The engagement pipeline adds review.received and review.response_received. Assignment-graded, quiz-completion and certificate events fire on the same pipeline. Templates ship with email, SMS, in-app and push variants."
    - question: "Are payouts handled in the platform?"
      answer: "The notification side ships with payout.processed and payout.failed templates on every channel. The actual payout calculation lives in the billing service — pricing, revenue share and payout amount are billing concerns — but the moment a payout moves or fails, the platform fires the right notification to the instructor automatically."
    - question: "How is the user marked as an instructor?"
      answer: "A boolean is_instructor flag on the user record carries the instructor type at the auth-service level. The flag is set when an admin invites a user as an instructor or assigns the instructor role. The flag drives the unique-ID prefix the platform generates per tenant, the visibility of the instructor in People → Instructors, and the role-based gating the admin sidebar applies."
    - question: "Is an instructor also a tenant role?"
      answer: "Yes. The instructor role lives in the tenant's RBAC system as a real role with a permission set, separate from the type flag. The admin sidebar reads the role to decide which menu items render, so an instructor signs into /admin and sees a teaching-scoped subset (courses, paths, cohorts, enrollments, learners, content bank, grading, bookings, communication and course analytics) without admin-only items like billing, audit or system settings."
    - question: "Can I run a guest-expert flow without giving them course-wide access?"
      answer: "Yes. Lesson-level assignment is the path. Add a single instructors row scoped to (entity_type='lesson', entity_id=<lessonID>) with the guest's user ID and a role of instructor. Permissions stay scoped to that lesson, so the guest sees only what they teach and the rest of the course is invisible to them."
    - question: "Does instructor data export?"
      answer: "Yes. The standard CSV export pipeline covers users — including the is_instructor flag — and every program (course, cohort, learning path) carries its instructor relations alongside its own metadata. Bulk export from People → Instructors is gated by user.export, and per-program instructor lists ride along on the course / path / cohort exports."
    - question: "What happens when an instructor is removed?"
      answer: "Soft delete. The instructors row is stamped with deleted_at and disappears from the active assignments, but the historical relationship is preserved. The user record itself is untouched, so a removed instructor on one course can keep their assignments on every other course they teach. Past notifications, past payouts and past course events stay intact for audit."
    - question: "Can two tenants share an instructor?"
      answer: "No — the platform is multi-tenant with hard isolation at the tenant_id boundary. Every instructors row carries a tenant_id, and queries always filter by it. If the same person teaches at two organizations on Edushade, they hold two user records (one per tenant) and two sets of instructor assignments. There is no cross-tenant view by design."
