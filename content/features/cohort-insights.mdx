faq:
  title: "Cohort Insights FAQ"
  description: "What teachers and program managers usually want to know about reading cohort data."
  items:
    - question: "What does a cohort actually contain?"
      answer: "A cohort is a time-bound batch of a course or learning path — start date, end date, weekly schedule, capacity and a list of enrolled learners. Cohort Insights gives you the analytics for that specific batch: how many enrolled, how far they've come, how many finished, plus the schedule and capacity so you read the numbers in context."
    - question: "Where do I find a cohort's analytics?"
      answer: "On the cohort's own page in admin. Open Resources → Cohorts → pick a cohort, and the Analytics tab is right there. You don't have to filter through a platform-wide dashboard to find one batch — every cohort has its own home with its own numbers."
    - question: "What metrics does each cohort show?"
      answer: "Enrollment count, completion rate, the cohort name and parent program, start and end dates, and weekly schedule. As the batch progresses, those numbers fill in — completed counts climb, the rate adjusts, the picture builds. The performance view rolls cohort-level engagement, satisfaction and ratings up too."
    - question: "How does seat utilisation work?"
      answer: "Each cohort can carry a max-enrollment cap. The dashboard shows enrolled out of capacity and remaining seats live, so you know at a glance whether to keep enrolling or close the gates. The platform respects the cap automatically — no accidental over-enrollment when a cohort fills up."
    - question: "Can I compare two cohorts of the same course?"
      answer: "Yes. Each cohort keeps its own metrics, so the comparison is open-two-tabs simple. The course performance view also lays out every cohort that's run that course as its own row, with the same metrics across all of them — completion, engagement, satisfaction, ratings, enrolled, completed. Differences pop the moment you see them side by side."
    - question: "What about comparing across multiple runs of the same program?"
      answer: "That's the point. Run a course as a spring cohort and an autumn cohort and you have a real before-and-after — same content, different surroundings. Run it three times and the pattern becomes signal, not noise. The cohort-by-cohort breakdown surfaces every run with its own numbers, so the trajectory is right there."
    - question: "Can I see the individual learners inside a cohort?"
      answer: "Yes. Every cohort has a Learners tab with the full enrolled list — name, progress, status, grade and last activity. Sort by any column. Open one learner for their full progress detail. From the same page you can send a notice or message a learner directly when the number tells you to."
    - question: "What if a cohort hasn't started yet?"
      answer: "Then it shows what it has — the enrollments rolling in, capacity, schedule. Once the cohort starts and learners begin engaging, the metrics fill in. The dashboard handles a cohort gracefully whether it's on day one or day fifty."
    - question: "Can I export the cohort's data?"
      answer: "Yes. The cohort's Learners list exports to CSV, and the platform-wide cohort listing has a bulk export too. Useful for cohort-by-cohort post-mortems, term-end reporting, and the kind of comparisons that benefit from being in a spreadsheet alongside other data."
    - question: "How do schedule fields work on a cohort?"
      answer: "A cohort carries a start date and an end date, plus a weekly schedule (the days and times the cohort runs, when relevant). Those fields are surfaced alongside the analytics so you read the numbers in the context of the calendar that produced them — \"completion is at 40% in week 3\" reads very differently from \"completion is at 40% in week 8\"."
    - question: "Can different cohorts have different instructors?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade's instructor model lets you assign instructors at the cohort level — so the spring cohort can have a different lead from the autumn cohort. Cohort Insights makes the comparison meaningful: same content, different instructor, real performance data on each."
    - question: "Is the data live?"
      answer: "Close to live. Enrollment counts update the moment someone joins. Completion rates update as learners finish. The cohort detail reflects the current state of the batch within minutes of the underlying activity."
    - question: "How is this different from Course Performance?"
      answer: "Course Performance is the catalogue view — every course on the platform with its overall numbers. Cohort Insights is the batch view — one specific run of a course. A course is a template; a cohort is a real moment in time. Both views are designed to be read together."
    - question: "Who can see Cohort Insights?"
      answer: "Admins by default, plus instructors with the cohort-view permission scoped to programs they teach. So an instructor running a single cohort sees their cohort's data; a platform admin sees every cohort. The role-based access keeps sensitive numbers in front of the right people."
    - question: "Will it work for big cohorts?"
      answer: "Yes. Cohort analytics are scoped to one batch's enrollments, so even a cohort with a thousand learners stays fast. The drill into the Learners tab is paginated and sortable so a long list is still readable."
