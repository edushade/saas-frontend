faq:
  title: "Assessments & Exams FAQ"
  description: "Specifics about how Edushade's quiz engine handles question types, grading, attempts, proctoring, merit lists and certificates."
  items:
    - question: "What is the difference between a quiz and an assessment in Edushade?"
      answer: "They live in the same builder. A quiz has a quiz_type field with four options — practice, graded, survey and assessment. The type drives the defaults: practice is for repetition without weight, graded counts toward marks, survey collects feedback without scoring, assessment is built for high-stakes exams with proctoring on by default."
    - question: "Which question types are supported?"
      answer: "Ten builders are live today: multiple choice (single answer), multiple choice (multiple answers), true or false, dropdown selection, fill in the blank (exact match), matching, short answer, long answer, open-ended and comprehension. Six are auto-gradable; four route to manual review."
    - question: "How does grading work?"
      answer: "Every question has a score and an optional negative score. When the attempt is submitted, auto-gradable questions resolve instantly using the answer key. Subjective questions land in a manual-grading queue where instructors award the score, optionally add partial credit and write feedback. Once all answers are graded, the attempt is marked graded and the learner sees results based on your visibility settings."
    - question: "Can I use negative marking?"
      answer: "Yes. Each question carries both a score and a negative_score. Set the negative score per question and Edushade subtracts it on a wrong answer. The default is zero, so negative marking is opt-in per question, not platform-wide."
    - question: "How are time limits enforced?"
      answer: "A quiz can carry a total time limit up to eight hours and an optional per-question time limit. You also get a warning_time_minutes value to alert the learner before the deadline, and a grace_period_minutes window at the end. Once the limit is up, the attempt auto-submits with the answers saved so far."
    - question: "Can learners retake a quiz?"
      answer: "Yes, controlled per quiz. Set max_attempts, a cooldown_period_hours and a retake_policy. Policies are immediate, after_cooldown, instructor_approval and no_retakes. Cooldowns can be set up to one week (168 hours), and the highest scoring attempt is kept on the record."
    - question: "What proctoring controls exist?"
      answer: "Full-screen mode, copy-paste disable, randomised question order, password protection, webcam requirement, screen-recording requirement and tab-switch detection — all per quiz, all switchable. Each toggle is opt-in, so a practice quiz can stay relaxed while a final exam locks down."
    - question: "Are questions or options shuffled?"
      answer: "Both, optionally. Toggle shuffle_questions and shuffle_options on the quiz, plus a per-question shuffle_options override. Each attempt stores its own shuffle order, so two learners never see the same arrangement and reviewers can replay the exact sequence the learner saw."
    - question: "Can a quiz be paused?"
      answer: "Yes when allow_pause is on. Learners can pause and resume within the time window. With pause off, the timer keeps running once the attempt starts, which is the right setting for proctored exams."
    - question: "How is a passing score set?"
      answer: "Per quiz, as a percentage between 0 and 100 (default 70). The attempt's percentage is compared against the passing score; passed is stored as a boolean on the attempt and drives certificate issuance and merit list eligibility."
    - question: "What is the merit list?"
      answer: "An automatic ranking of attempts on a quiz. Two visibility flags control it: show_merit_list_to_learner and show_other_merit_list. Use them together to keep the merit list private, show only the learner their position, or publish the full ranking to everyone who attempted."
    - question: "Are certificates issued for assessments?"
      answer: "Yes. Toggle certificate_enabled on the quiz. When a learner crosses the passing score, a certificate is issued automatically with their score, percentage and timestamp captured on the attempt."
    - question: "Can learners flag a problem with a question?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade ships a question report flow with five reasons — content clarity, visual quality, typography, technical and other — and a status pipeline of pending, reviewed, resolved and dismissed. Reports stay attached to the question for revision history."
    - question: "What attempt data is stored?"
      answer: "Each attempt carries the attempt number, started-at, submitted-at, time-spent, total and obtained scores, percentage, passed flag, status (in_progress, submitted, graded, expired or abandoned), auto-graded and manual-grading-required flags, instructor feedback, IP address, user agent, full submission data and the per-attempt shuffle order. All exportable as CSV from the admin."
    - question: "Can I export attempts and quiz data?"
      answer: "Yes. Quizzes export with title, description, type, status and access fields. Attempts export with full scoring, timing, learner identity, grading workflow fields and submission counts. Use either to back up data, audit a session or hand records to a registrar."
    - question: "How big can a quiz get?"
      answer: "Up to 200 questions per quiz and up to 20 options per question. Question text can be 5 to 5000 characters and option text up to 500. The validation matches the way real exams are written, not arbitrary limits."
