faq:
  title: "Scholarships FAQ"
  description: "What founders, admins and program managers want to know before they offer financial aid through the platform."
  items:
    - question: "What can a scholarship apply to?"
      answer: "A specific course, a learning path or a cohort. So you can offer a scholarship for one course, for a longer learning journey, or for a single batch of a course running on a specific schedule. The choice is yours when you create the scholarship."
    - question: "How do learners discover scholarships?"
      answer: "Each scholarship has its own public page on your site, with the title, description, thumbnail and percentage on display. You can also link to it from the relevant course page, embed it in a website-builder block, or share the URL directly. Discovery is part of the experience — not a hidden coupon code."
    - question: "How do they apply?"
      answer: "They tap apply on the scholarship's page and fill in the application form you've attached. The form is built in your forms builder, so you control the questions — financial circumstances, why this matters to them, anything else relevant. Their answers come back to you with the application."
    - question: "What's the difference between a fixed and an 'up to' scholarship?"
      answer: "A fixed scholarship gives every approved applicant the same percentage off — say, 30% for everyone who's approved. An 'up to' scholarship lets you decide the percentage per applicant when you approve. Useful when you want to be generous with some and reasonable with others, based on what they've shared."
    - question: "Can two applicants get different percentages on the same scholarship?"
      answer: "Yes — that's exactly what 'up to' is for. The scholarship sets the maximum. The percentage you actually grant on each application can be anywhere from 1% to that maximum. Each application carries its own granted percentage, so the record stays clear."
    - question: "What happens after I approve an application?"
      answer: "The learner gets a scholarship-approved notification on the channels they've enabled — email, SMS, in-app, push — with your message and their granted percentage. They can then enroll in the program at the discounted price, with the scholarship attached to their purchase. Done in a moment, no extra steps."
    - question: "What does rejecting feel like for the learner?"
      answer: "They get a scholarship-rejected notification with the reason you wrote and any admin note you added. Honest, kind, on the record. They can usually re-apply for a different scholarship, or apply again later if the original criteria changes."
    - question: "What are revoke policies?"
      answer: "Rules that protect the spirit of the scholarship after it's been granted. You define them on the scholarship itself — \"quiz score drops below 60%\", \"inactive for 30 days\", \"failed this specific quiz\". When a condition fires, the platform revokes the scholarship automatically and notifies the learner. The discount goes to people who actually engage, not people who quietly disappear."
    - question: "Can I revoke a scholarship manually too?"
      answer: "Yes. From the application's detail view, you can revoke at any time, with a reason and access control options for the underlying program. The history log keeps the full story, so the audit trail is intact whether the revocation was automatic or human-driven."
    - question: "How does the discount actually apply?"
      answer: "When the learner enrolls, the platform applies the granted percentage to the program's price as a real discount. They pay the reduced amount, the order shows the discount line, and the scholarship is linked to the purchase so the connection is visible if you ever look back."
    - question: "What if a scholarship has a deadline?"
      answer: "Use the start and end dates when you create it. The public page hides the scholarship outside those dates, applications stop being accepted, and the offer simply sleeps until you bring it back. Useful for end-of-year offers, intake-specific scholarships, or anything time-bound."
    - question: "Can I run bulk operations?"
      answer: "Yes. The Scholarship Applications list supports bulk export to CSV for record-keeping or external review, plus bulk delete when you're cleaning up an old programme. There's also a bulk eligibility check that runs the criteria against many applications at once — useful when an intake brings a wave of applications and you want to triage fast."
    - question: "Are decisions audited?"
      answer: "Yes. Every approval, rejection and revocation lands on the application's history log with the actor, the timestamp, and the reason or note. The platform-wide audit trail captures it too. So the path each application took — from submission through every decision — stays readable months later."
    - question: "Are scholarships notifications opt-out for learners?"
      answer: "Approval and rejection notifications are transactional, so they always go out. Reminder-style notifications (e.g. application nudges) respect each learner's notification preferences per channel. The right messages reach the right people without overwhelming anyone."
    - question: "Is the data tenant-isolated?"
      answer: "Hard-isolated. Every scholarship and every application belongs to a single tenant. Admins on one organisation never see another organisation's scholarships, applications or applicants — even by accident. The discoverable public page is yours alone."
    - question: "Who can manage scholarships on my team?"
      answer: "Anyone with the scholarship permissions — view, create, update, delete and the bulk export permissions — set in your Roles & Permissions builder. So you can grant scholarship management to a financial aid coordinator without giving them the rest of the admin. Common patterns: a dedicated 'Aid Officer' role with scholarship.* and the form-view permission, plus read access to the relevant courses."
