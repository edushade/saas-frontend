faq:
  title: "Live Bookings FAQ"
  description: "How meeting plans, availability, mediums, integrations, the booking lifecycle, group seats, requests and paid sessions actually work inside Edushade."
  items:
    - question: "What is a meeting plan in Edushade?"
      answer: "A meeting plan is a bookable template — the equivalent of a Cal.com event type or a Calendly event. It carries a title, a unique slug, a timezone, a duration, an optional description and color, a medium, weekly availability rules, plus the policy fields that govern buffers, notice, group capacity, request approvals and payment gating. Every booking is created against a meeting plan."
    - question: "How does the public booking page work?"
      answer: "Each meeting plan gets its own public page at /bookings/{slug} on your branded site, plus a directory at /bookings with category filters and search. The detail page renders a 6-week calendar with a timezone picker, a time-slot grid for the selected day, and a booking form for invitee + optional guests. Slots are generated in UTC and rendered in the invitee's local timezone."
    - question: "What meeting mediums are supported?"
      answer: "Five: Google Meet, Zoom, In Person, Phone Call and No Location. Google Meet auto-allocates a room on the calendar event. Zoom creates a real Zoom meeting and stores the join URL plus password on the booking. In Person carries a physical address. Phone Call carries a phone number. No Location is a no-op for asynchronous mediums."
    - question: "What integrations are required?"
      answer: "Google Calendar is the central integration — every external artifact (Meet rooms, Zoom calendar wrapping, in-person calendar invites) flows through Google Calendar. Connect it once via OAuth from Admin → Booking → Integrations. Add Zoom on the Conference tab if you run Zoom sessions. Google Meet does not need a separate connection — it ships as a has_meet flag on the same Google integration."
    - question: "What is the difference between request-based and instant booking?"
      answer: "Instant plans use weekly availability — invitees pick a slot and the booking is confirmed on submit. Request-based plans skip availability rules: the invitee proposes one or more preferred slots, the booking lands in the requested status, and the admin reviews and confirms a final date and time. Calendar events and join links are created at confirmation time, not at submission."
    - question: "How do paid sessions work?"
      answer: "Toggle is_collect_payment_after_confirmation on the meeting plan and add a price record in the billing service. New bookings against that plan land in the pending status, the booking:expiry job is scheduled, and the booking transitions to in_schedule the moment a successful payment lands. Unpaid bookings expire automatically after pending_expiry_minutes — no manual cleanup."
    - question: "Can a single slot host multiple invitees?"
      answer: "Yes. Set group_meeting_spot to 2 or more on the meeting plan and the public availability feed exposes spots_left per slot. The first invitee who books creates the schedule; subsequent invitees on the same slot are added as additional Invitee rows on the same booking. When spots_left hits zero, the slot disappears from the calendar."
    - question: "What policy controls ship with each plan?"
      answer: "Buffer before, buffer after, minimum notice, max meetings per day, cancel-before-minutes, reschedule-before-minutes, group_meeting_spot, additional guests, pending expiry, lock-meeting-plan-timezone and has-invitee-call. Most are nullable integers; -1 is the explicit no-deadline sentinel on cancel/reschedule. Every policy is enforced server-side, not just in the UI."
    - question: "What statuses does a booking go through?"
      answer: "Six: in_schedule (confirmed), requested (approval-waiting), pending (payment-waiting), rescheduled (time changed), canceled (stopped) and expired (unpaid bookings that ran out the timer). The admin schedules dashboard filters on each, and reschedule and cancel both mirror to the connected Google Calendar event so attendees never see a stale invite."
    - question: "Which notifications are sent?"
      answer: "Eight templates ship with the platform: booking confirmed, booking cancelled, meeting scheduled, meeting reminder, meeting started, meeting rescheduled, meeting cancelled and meeting recording available. Each has email, SMS, in-app and push variants, and the templates are editable from the notification settings UI — no external email tool, no Zap, no copy-paste between systems."
    - question: "Do invitees need to sign in to book?"
      answer: "Yes — the POST /meeting-schedules endpoint that creates a booking sits behind the user-auth middleware, so the invitee must be authenticated. Discovery (/bookings, /bookings/{slug}, the availability feed) is fully public; only the actual booking submission requires a signed-in invitee. Anyone can browse the calendar without an account."
    - question: "Can I customize the public booking page?"
      answer: "Yes. The booking-service ships per-user BookingPageSetting records — title, layout, background color, thumbnail, cover image — surfaced on the public page. The base path itself (/bookings, /book, /appointments, /schedule) is configurable from the website settings, so the public URL fits whatever language your team uses."
    - question: "Who on my team can manage bookings?"
      answer: "The booking module ships its own RBAC permissions — meeting_plan.create, meeting_plan.update, meeting_plan.delete, meeting_plan.view, plus the schedule.* and setting.* groups for the bookings dashboard and integration config. Assign them to a role and any user with that role inherits the access, scoped to the tenant."
    - question: "How does timezone handling work?"
      answer: "Slots are stored on the wire in UTC and as minutes-from-midnight in the plan's timezone for availability rules. The public page renders slots in the invitee's local timezone by default, with a 21-zone dropdown for overrides. Lock the picker by flipping has_lock_meeting_plan_timezone on the plan — useful when the time of a session is the time of the session, regardless of where the invitee opens the link."
    - question: "Can I export bookings or hand them to another service?"
      answer: "Yes. The schedules dashboard backs onto a CSV export, and the booking-service exposes a /v1/system/* surface that the billing and notification services already consume — so any internal service with a system token can read schedules, prices and invitees without going through user auth."
