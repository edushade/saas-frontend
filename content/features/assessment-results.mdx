faq:
  title: "Assessment Results FAQ"
  description: "What teachers usually want to know before they trust a platform with grading."
  items:
    - question: "What does an attempt actually capture?"
      answer: "Everything — the start time, the submission time, the time spent, the obtained score versus total, the percentage, pass or fail, plus per-question status (correct, incorrect, partial, skipped, pending review). The IP and device are also captured for audit. Every detail you'd need to reconstruct what happened."
    - question: "Which questions auto-grade?"
      answer: "Most of them. Multiple choice with one or multiple correct answers, true/false, matching pairs, ordering or sequencing, fill-in-the-blank with multiple acceptable answers and per-blank case sensitivity, numeric answers with a tolerance range, math expressions, and hotspot click-on-image. The platform handles all of those without an instructor lifting a finger."
    - question: "What about free-form questions?"
      answer: "They land in the grading queue. The instructor opens the attempt, reads the response, scores it, and leaves feedback. Save, and the platform recalculates the overall percentage and fires the quiz-graded notification to the learner so they know it's been reviewed."
    - question: "Can a learner retake a quiz?"
      answer: "If you've allowed it, yes. Each quiz has a max-attempts setting and an optional cooldown between retakes. Every attempt is its own record — the platform never overwrites an old attempt with a new one — so you always have the history. The result you grade against can be the latest, the highest or whichever your program rules say."
    - question: "How is the percentage calculated?"
      answer: "Obtained score divided by total score, times one hundred. The total score is the sum of every question's marks. Partial credit is supported on question types that allow it (matching pairs, fill-in-the-blank with per-blank scoring, multi-answer multiple choice), so a learner who gets two of four blanks right doesn't get zero on that question."
    - question: "Can I see how the rest of the class did?"
      answer: "Yes. The merit list shows rank, score and percentile across every learner who took the quiz. If you've turned on the leaderboard, learners see it too — useful for healthy competition. The progress dashboard's quiz comparison view goes a layer deeper, surfacing question-by-question stats so you can spot which questions tripped the class up."
    - question: "Will learners see their answers right away?"
      answer: "Up to you. The quiz settings let you decide whether to show results immediately on submission, whether to reveal the correct answers, whether to show explanations per question and whether to allow review of the attempt afterwards. You can keep it tight for an exam and open for a practice quiz on the same platform."
    - question: "What about quiz security for higher-stakes exams?"
      answer: "Quizzes can require full-screen mode, block copy-paste, require webcam or screen recording, randomise question and answer order per attempt, and flag tab switching when it happens. The shuffle order is stored per attempt, so you can review exactly the version a learner saw if a question is contested."
    - question: "Do learners get notified when their result is in?"
      answer: "Yes. Five notifications fire automatically — completed (the attempt was submitted), passed (they cleared the threshold), failed (they didn't), all attempts exhausted (they've used their last try), and graded (an instructor finished a manual review). Each goes out by email, in-app, SMS or push, depending on what they have on."
    - question: "Can I leave instructor feedback on an attempt?"
      answer: "Yes — there's a feedback field on every attempt. Whatever you write is included in the quiz-graded notification, so the learner reads it the same day. Pair it with the per-question explanation field on the quiz itself and learners get both the right answer and the why."
    - question: "What if a quiz issues a certificate?"
      answer: "Certificates fire the moment a learner crosses the passing score on a graded quiz. The certificate is unique-numbered and verifiable through the platform's verification code, and the learner gets the certificate-issued notification automatically."
    - question: "Can I export results?"
      answer: "Yes. Per-quiz attempt lists and per-cohort merit lists both export to CSV. Use them for grade-book reconciliation, an external transcript system, or a board update."
    - question: "Does this work for surveys too?"
      answer: "Yes — surveys are one of the four quiz types Edushade ships, alongside practice, graded and assessment. Surveys collect responses without grading, so the result page on a survey shows the response distribution rather than a percentage. Same engine, different mode."
    - question: "What can I do with the question-level data?"
      answer: "More than you'd think. The progress dashboard surfaces a quiz comparison view that lays out every learner's score on every quiz side by side — the question half the class missed shows up immediately. Use it to plan the next session, refine the question, or simply re-teach the topic with the data in hand."
    - question: "Is it auditable?"
      answer: "Yes. Every grading action — manual scoring, feedback edits, attempt resets — lands in the audit log with the actor, the attempt and the timestamp. Combined with the per-attempt IP and device capture, the platform gives you a paper trail you can defend in front of an exam board or a regulator."
