faq:
  title: "Notifications FAQ"
  description: "How notifications work in Edushade — channels, providers, templates, settings, tracking and the differences from announcements."
  items:
    - question: "What is the notification system in Edushade?"
      answer: "A dedicated service that sends transactional, marketing and announcement messages across seven channels. It owns its own templates, provider configs, queue, tracking and settings — separate from the platform's other services, so a billing event and a learning event ride the same engine."
    - question: "Which channels are supported?"
      answer: "Seven: email, SMS, push (APNs for iOS, FCM for Android and web), WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack and Discord. Each is a first-class channel with its own template body field, its own provider stack and its own delivery log."
    - question: "Which providers can I plug in?"
      answer: "Email: SendGrid, Mailgun, AWS SES and generic SMTP. SMS: Twilio, Vonage, Plivo, BulkSMS24, HTTPSms and AWS SNS. Push: APNs and FCM. Chat: native providers for WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack and Discord. Each tenant configures its own keys per provider."
    - question: "Can I run multiple providers on one channel?"
      answer: "Yes. Add multiple provider configs per channel and rank them by priority — lower number is higher priority. The dispatcher tries them in order, so a SendGrid outage falls over to AWS SES without engineering work. Each provider also carries its own rate limit (messages per minute) to honour vendor caps."
    - question: "How many events come pre-wired?"
      answer: "110 default templates across fourteen categories: auth, enrollment, learning progress, course management, assignments, quizzes, certificates, billing, e-commerce, engagement and community, booking and live sessions, cohorts, tenant admin, and file management. Every event the platform fires has a sendable template behind it."
    - question: "What does a template look like?"
      answer: "A single template carries content for every channel: email_subject, email_body_html and email_design_json (for the visual editor); sms_body; push_title and push_body; and dedicated body fields for WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack and Discord. Plus a list of dynamic variables and a version history."
    - question: "Are templates versioned?"
      answer: "Yes. Every save creates a TemplateVersion entry with the full content and the editor's user ID. Roll back to a previous version, compare two side by side, or audit who changed what — all from the template detail page."
    - question: "What categories of notification exist?"
      answer: "Three template types — Transactional, Marketing and Announcement — plus a routing category called Ephemeral for OTP, password reset and other one-shot security messages. The category drives whether the message is rate-limited, suppressed by quiet hours, or counted toward a campaign."
    - question: "Can I disable specific notifications?"
      answer: "Yes, at two granularities. Group-level toggles disable a whole category (e.g. 'all engagement notifications off'). Per-event overrides target a single event key (e.g. 'turn off discussion.mentioned but keep discussion.created on'). The per-event override always wins over the group toggle when both are set."
    - question: "How do quiet hours work?"
      answer: "Tenant-level quiet hours carry a start time, an end time and a timezone. Inside the window, notifications are held and rescheduled to the next available moment — overnight ranges (e.g. 22:00-06:00) are handled correctly. An apply-to-marketing-only flag lets transactional messages bypass the window when they need to."
    - question: "Are there rate limits?"
      answer: "Two layers. Provider-level rate limit caps messages per minute per provider — for vendor caps. Tenant-level marketing rate limit caps messages per learner per channel — for inbox health. Settings cover marketing per day and marketing per week, and an exclude-transactional flag keeps transactional sends out of the count."
    - question: "What does delivery and open tracking look like?"
      answer: "Every send writes a row to the delivery log; every observed event (sent, delivered, opened, clicked, bounced, failed) writes to the notification_events table with channel, provider, IP and user-agent. Webhooks from the providers update these records, so an opened email and a bounced SMS both surface in the same view."
    - question: "Is there a suppression list?"
      answer: "Yes. Suppressed contacts are kept in their own list with a reason — BOUNCE, COMPLAINT, UNSUBSCRIBE, GDPR_DELETION — and a source — USER, ADMIN, SYSTEM. The dispatcher checks suppression before every send, so a bounced address never gets retried, and a GDPR deletion is honoured platform-wide."
    - question: "What about marketing campaigns?"
      answer: "The same engine drives campaigns. A Campaign points at a Segment (dynamic or static), a Template, a list of channels and a schedule (immediate, scheduled or recurring with a recurrence rule). Statistics — sent, delivered, opened, clicked, bounced, failed — are tracked per campaign and per contact."
    - question: "How is the learner inbox built?"
      answer: "The learner sidebar carries a Bell with an unread badge (capped at 9+). Click it and a sheet opens with the in-app notification list, mark-as-read on click, mark-all-as-read in one tap, and infinite-scroll pagination. Notifications with an action_url navigate when clicked; ones without just dismiss."
    - question: "How is this different from Announcements?"
      answer: "Announcements (notices) live in their own service and are written by humans for a chosen audience — a course, a cohort, a role. Notifications are system-generated by events the platform already fires. The two intersect: when an instructor publishes an announcement, the notification engine fires announcement.published to the right learners through their preferred channels."
    - question: "Are webhooks supported?"
      answer: "Yes. Outbound webhook events fire on notification.sent, notification.delivered, notification.failed, notification.opened, notification.clicked, plus campaign.started, campaign.completed and campaign.paused. Wire them into your data warehouse, ops dashboard or any external system that needs to know."
