faq:
  title: "Workshops FAQ"
  description: "How workshops work inside Edushade — sessions, registrations, attendance, recurring schedules and how they differ from courses and cohorts."
  items:
    - question: "What is a workshop in Edushade?"
      answer: "A workshop is a standalone session with its own start time, registration deadline, capacity cap, speakers and access rules. It is not a sub-resource of a course — you can run a one-off webinar, a recurring office hour or an in-person bootcamp without ever creating a course around it."
    - question: "How is a workshop different from a course or a cohort?"
      answer: "A course is a curriculum of lessons, quizzes and assignments that learners progress through over time. A cohort is a scheduled batch of a course or learning path. A workshop is a single session — one room, one date, one outcome. Workshops live alongside courses on the platform, not inside them."
    - question: "What session formats are supported?"
      answer: "Three formats: live, recorded and offline. Live workshops integrate with a meeting provider and capture a join URL. Recorded workshops play a video URL at the scheduled time. Offline workshops capture a venue and use the same registration and attendance flow as the other two."
    - question: "Which meeting providers can I connect?"
      answer: "Google Meet, Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Jitsi and a generic other option for anything else with a join URL. The provider is picked per workshop, so you can run one session on Zoom and the next on Meet without changing anything platform-wide."
    - question: "Can I cap how many people register?"
      answer: "Yes. Each workshop has a max-participants field that accepts anywhere from 0 (unlimited) up to 50,000. Once the cap is hit, registration closes automatically. Registration count and participant count are tracked separately, so you can see who signed up versus who actually showed up."
    - question: "How does registration work?"
      answer: "Registration is required by default and captures name, email, phone, company and designation, plus UTM source, medium, campaign, term and content for marketing attribution. You can also bulk-import participants by user IDs or by file, and export the full list as CSV, XLSX or JSON whenever you need to."
    - question: "Can a workshop be recurring?"
      answer: "Yes. Turn on recurring and pick a frequency — daily, weekly, monthly or yearly — with an interval, an end date and a max-occurrence count. Each occurrence becomes its own instance with its own status, recording and attendee numbers. A single date can be overridden for a one-off exception without changing the parent series."
    - question: "How is attendance tracked?"
      answer: "Each participant carries a status — registered, attended, missed or cancelled — plus joined-at and left-at timestamps. When a learner clicks join from the dashboard, Edushade records the join time and updates the status. For offline sessions, status can be updated manually or in bulk from the participants table."
    - question: "Can I charge for a workshop?"
      answer: "Yes. Workshops can carry one or more pricing options, and each participant record stores the chosen pricing and a payment status — pending, paid, failed or refunded. Free, paid and mixed-pricing workshops are all supported."
    - question: "What about chat, Q&A and other interactive features?"
      answer: "Live workshops have allow-chat and allow-Q-and-A toggles that decide whether those tools are enabled inside the meeting provider you connect. The interaction itself happens in the provider; Edushade is where you decide who gets in, what is on, and what happens before and after the room opens."
    - question: "Can I add multiple speakers to one workshop?"
      answer: "Yes. Workshops support a many-speaker model with a primary-speaker flag — useful when you have a host plus guest experts on the same session. Speakers are selected from your platform users and surface on the registration page automatically."
    - question: "What happens after a workshop ends?"
      answer: "Drop the recording URL in and the workshop page turns into the replay page. Recorded events open the recording on the published date, and live events surface a recording link to participants once you publish it. Attendance, join times and the full participant list stay on the workshop record for reporting."
    - question: "Can I export workshop data?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade ships full export for both workshops and their participants — title, schedule, registration counts, participant counts, attendance status, payment status and UTM tracking included. Bulk import for participants is also supported, so you can pull a list out, edit it offline, and push it back."
    - question: "What states can a workshop be in?"
      answer: "Workshops move through draft, published, completed and cancelled. Recurring instances carry their own status — scheduled, live, completed or cancelled — so the parent series and the individual runs can move independently when something changes mid-stream."
