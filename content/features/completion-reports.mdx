faq:
  title: "Completion Reports FAQ"
  description: "What people actually ask when they need completion data they can stand behind."
  items:
    - question: "What does 'completion' mean here?"
      answer: "A learner has finished all the curriculum items required by the course or path — lessons watched, quizzes passed at the configured threshold, assignments turned in. The platform marks them complete the moment the last requirement is satisfied. Soft requirements are honoured; you don't get marked complete on a half-done course."
    - question: "What four numbers are at the top of the page?"
      answer: "Overall completion (the share who finished), in-progress (the share working through it now), not-started (the share who enrolled but haven't moved), and average time to complete (how long the typical journey takes). Each one shows the current period next to the previous period, with the percentage change."
    - question: "Why is the previous-period comparison useful?"
      answer: "Because raw numbers don't tell you whether things are getting better. \"42% completion\" sounds OK or terrible depending on whether last term was 50% or 30%. The comparison is built into every tile so the read isn't \"how are we doing\", it's \"are we doing better\"."
    - question: "Can I filter to a specific cohort?"
      answer: "Yes. The filter row at the top lets you pick a cohort, a program and a date range. The whole report — KPIs, trend chart, by-level breakdown, recent completions — rescopes to that slice. Term-by-term and program-by-program comparisons are a dropdown, not a separate tool."
    - question: "What's the trend chart actually showing?"
      answer: "Three lines across the calendar — completed, in-progress, not-started — week by week. You can see when the curve starts climbing, where it plateaus, and how long the tail of stragglers lasts. Useful for understanding the shape of a cohort, not just the final number."
    - question: "What's the by-level breakdown?"
      answer: "Completion percentage broken out by course difficulty — beginner, intermediate, advanced. Useful for spotting where the curve gets steep. Maybe your beginner courses crush it but your advanced ones drop off — the chart makes it visible without an analyst."
    - question: "What does 'recent completions' show?"
      answer: "A live list of learners who just finished a program — name, course, the day they completed. Use it for a shout-out in the cohort channel, time the certificate handover, or celebrate a moment that's easy to lose in a dashboard. The list also exports cleanly when an auditor asks for the records."
    - question: "Can I export the report?"
      answer: "Yes — one click. The CSV lands with everything currently on the page (the filtered KPIs, trend, by-level, recent completions) ready to send to finance, HR, an accreditor or a board. No reformatting required."
    - question: "Does this work for learning paths?"
      answer: "Yes. Completion reports cover both single courses and learning paths. A path completion fires the same way as a course completion — the platform recognises when all required courses inside the path are done and marks the path complete."
    - question: "How is completion related to certificates?"
      answer: "Tightly. The same event that marks a learner complete also fires the certificate (when certificates are enabled on the course). The certificate snapshot stores the learner's grade, completion date and total time spent — useful for accreditation and for the learner's own record."
    - question: "Can I see who hasn't started?"
      answer: "Yes. The not-started number on the KPI tile is clickable — drill in and you'll see a list of learners who enrolled but never opened a lesson. That's your re-engagement list. A friendly nudge often turns half of them into completers."
    - question: "What if a learner completes, then comes back to revisit a lesson?"
      answer: "They stay marked complete. The platform doesn't undo a completion when someone goes back to revisit content — the completion event is tied to the moment they crossed the threshold, with its own timestamp. Time-spent keeps adding up if they keep engaging, but the report still shows them as complete."
    - question: "Is the data live?"
      answer: "Close to live. Completion events update the report's underlying numbers within a few minutes of the moment a learner crosses the line. The trend chart smooths to weekly grain so day-to-day noise doesn't drown the signal."
    - question: "Who can see Completion Reports?"
      answer: "Admins by default, plus instructors with the analytics-view permission scoped to their programs. The view is gated by role, so a teaching assistant can see the cohorts they teach while platform admins see the whole picture."
    - question: "How is this different from Progress Tracking?"
      answer: "Progress Tracking is the in-flight view — where every learner is right now, at the lesson level. Completion Reports is the outcome view — how many crossed the line, how long it took, how this term compares to last. Different questions, same source data."
