faq:
  title: "Progress Tracking FAQ"
  description: "The questions teachers and admins ask the first time they sit down with the dashboard."
  items:
    - question: "What gets tracked?"
      answer: "Everything a learner does inside the course — lessons opened, video positions, time spent, quiz attempts, assignment submissions and downloaded files. Each item carries its own progress record so the picture is detailed, not vague."
    - question: "Is the data real-time?"
      answer: "Yes. Progress updates the moment a learner finishes a lesson, submits a quiz or turns in an assignment. There's no overnight job to wait for. Open the dashboard and you're seeing the latest number."
    - question: "What does 'progress' actually mean for a course?"
      answer: "It's the share of curriculum items the learner has completed — lessons finished, quizzes passed, assignments turned in. So 60% on a course means they've cleared 60% of the items inside it, not that they finished 60% of one video."
    - question: "Can I see exactly where someone is, not just the percentage?"
      answer: "Yes. Open the learner from the cohort or course view and you'll see every lesson, quiz and assignment with its own status — completed, in progress, not started or paused — plus time spent and the last activity timestamp."
    - question: "What about videos — does it remember where they paused?"
      answer: "Yes. Each video lesson stores the last position, so when a learner comes back, they pick up where they left off. Time spent on the lesson is captured separately, so a five-minute video they revisited three times shows fifteen minutes of engagement, not five."
    - question: "Do learners see their own progress?"
      answer: "Absolutely. Their dashboard at /dashboard shows every course they're in, with progress bars, time-spent counters, what's coming next and a continue-learning button that drops them right back where they paused. It's the same data the admin sees, just scoped to them."
    - question: "Can I roll progress up to a cohort or section?"
      answer: "Yes. The dashboard has a section picker and a cohort filter. Choose either and the histogram, the trend line and the student-detail table re-scope to that slice. A TA running one batch sees their batch, not the whole program."
    - question: "Does completion trigger anything automatically?"
      answer: "Yes — and it should. The certificate fires the moment a learner crosses the passing grade, the learner gets a celebration, and the instructor gets a heads-up so the moment isn't lost in a dashboard. Milestone cheers along the way are configurable per course."
    - question: "What if someone goes quiet?"
      answer: "The platform tracks last activity on every enrollment. If a learner hasn't been around for a while, an inactive-reminder notification fires automatically. You can also sort the cohort table by last activity in the admin and reach out personally — sometimes a five-minute message saves a learner."
    - question: "Does it work for both self-paced and cohort courses?"
      answer: "Yes. The same progress engine powers both. A self-paced learner moves at their own speed — every step still tracked. A cohort moves on a schedule — the trend line reads it as a clean wave. The dashboard handles either shape without a separate setup."
    - question: "Can I compare quiz performance across the cohort?"
      answer: "Yes. The student-detail table on the progress dashboard lays out every learner's score on every quiz side by side, so you can spot the quiz that's tripping people up — or the section that's coasting through. It's the question \"who got which question wrong?\" answered in one view."
    - question: "What about learning paths — does progress chain across courses?"
      answer: "Yes. A learning path's progress aggregates across the courses inside it. The platform also fires a learning-path-started notification when the first course begins, a course-unlocked event when the next one becomes available, and a learning-path-completed celebration when the whole journey is done."
    - question: "Is the data exportable?"
      answer: "Yes. Every cohort and program-level table supports CSV export — pull the entire progress snapshot for a class, a cohort or a learning path in one click. Useful for a quick review with a stakeholder, a regulator or a compliance team."
    - question: "Will this work for a really large program?"
      answer: "Yes. The progress engine scales to thousands of learners per program. Real-time updates fire on the action's own thread; reads come from indexed data so the dashboard stays quick. Filtering by section or cohort keeps even the biggest programs readable."
