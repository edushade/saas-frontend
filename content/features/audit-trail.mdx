faq:
  title: "Audit Trail FAQ"
  description: "The questions that come up the first time you sit down with the audit logs."
  items:
    - question: "What's the difference between the Audit Log and the Activity Log?"
      answer: "The Audit Log is for the privileged changes your team makes — creating a course, changing a price, assigning a role, approving a refund. The Activity Log is for everything your learners do — signing in, enrolling, opening a lesson, attempting a quiz. Both live in the same admin view, but they answer different questions."
    - question: "What does each entry actually contain?"
      answer: "The person who did it (name and avatar), what they did (action), what they did it to (the resource), where in the platform it happened (module), the date and time, the IP address and the device. For updates, you also see what the value was before and after, so the change reads as a clear before-and-after."
    - question: "Do I need to set this up?"
      answer: "No. The audit and activity streams are on by default the moment your tenant is created. Every change made through the admin and every action your learners take is recorded automatically. There's no opt-in step, no manual instrumentation, no extra config."
    - question: "What kinds of changes get audited?"
      answer: "The privileged ones across every part of the platform — courses, learning paths, cohorts, lessons, quizzes, assignments, enrollments, prices, scholarships, transactions, roles, permissions, users, notices, notifications, the website builder. If a member of your team changes something material, it's on the record."
    - question: "Can I see what changed, not just that something changed?"
      answer: "Yes. When the action is an update, the entry stores the value before and after. So if someone changed a course's passing grade from 70% to 75%, the log shows you both numbers right there. You don't need to dig through commits or compare snapshots."
    - question: "Does impersonation get tracked?"
      answer: "Yes — and clearly. When a support admin signs in as a learner to debug, the actions show up against the learner's account, but the entry also names the admin who was driving. So an account never quietly does something its owner didn't do."
    - question: "Can my learners see their own activity?"
      answer: "Yes. Each user can pull up their own activity history from their profile — sign-ins, enrollments, lessons opened, quiz attempts. It's the same data the admin sees in the Activity Log, just scoped to them. Useful for transparency and for resolving \"did I really do that?\" questions."
    - question: "Can I filter to find what I need?"
      answer: "Yes. Both logs share a filter row — person, action, resource, module and date range. You can mix and match as many as you like; the list narrows as you add. \"Show me everything Sara did in billing last week\" is a four-click query."
    - question: "What about exports?"
      answer: "There's an Export button on the audit log view. It pulls the same set you've filtered to, in either CSV or JSON, and gives you a file ready to send to legal, finance, an external auditor or your own data warehouse."
    - question: "How long are entries kept?"
      answer: "As long as you want. Each stream has its own retention window — you might keep admin Audit Logs for eighteen months and Activity Logs for ninety days, or any other split that makes sense. Older entries are retired automatically on the schedule you pick. Adjust the window any time."
    - question: "Who on my team can see the audit logs?"
      answer: "Admins with the audit-read permission. Instructors don't see the audit log by default — it's an admin-only surface — but if your organization wants a custom role with audit visibility (a compliance officer, an auditor), you can create one in Roles & Permissions and grant audit-read to it."
    - question: "Is the data tenant-isolated?"
      answer: "Yes. Every entry belongs to a single tenant, and queries always filter by it. An admin on one organization can never see another organization's audit history, even by accident."
    - question: "Will turning audit logging on slow down the platform?"
      answer: "No. The platform writes audit and activity events on a separate stream from the action itself, so the user-facing flow doesn't wait on the log being persisted. The recording happens in the background."
    - question: "What if something goes wrong with logging?"
      answer: "The user's action still completes. Logging is a side channel — it never stops a learner from finishing a lesson or an admin from saving a setting. The platform retries the write and surfaces gaps in the operations dashboard so you know if a stream needs attention."
