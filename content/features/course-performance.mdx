faq:
  title: "Course Performance FAQ"
  description: "What people ask the first time they look at the catalogue's numbers."
  items:
    - question: "What does 'completion rate' mean here?"
      answer: "The share of enrolled learners on a course who have crossed the finish line — completed every required curriculum item, passed any required quizzes, turned in any required assignments. The headline tile averages it across the whole catalogue; the per-course row shows it for each course."
    - question: "What is 'engagement' on this page?"
      answer: "A blended score capturing how active the enrolled learners are on a course — how many are showing up, how much time they're putting in, how often they return. It isn't a click count or a login number; it's a synthesised read on whether the course is keeping people interested."
    - question: "Where does 'satisfaction' come from?"
      answer: "From learner reviews and ratings. The platform aggregates the star ratings on each course and rolls them into a percentage, so the satisfaction column reflects what learners actually said — not a proxy. The twelve-month trend smooths week-to-week noise into a readable line."
    - question: "What counts as a 'high-performing' course?"
      answer: "A course whose completion, engagement and satisfaction all sit comfortably above the catalogue's median — the courses that consistently work. The count gives you a quick read on how many of your titles fall into that bucket, with the growth percent showing whether the bucket is filling up over time."
    - question: "Why is the previous-period comparison built into every tile?"
      answer: "Because raw numbers don't tell you whether things are getting better. \"58% completion\" sounds OK or terrible depending on whether last quarter was 50% or 65%. The comparison pulls the read into focus — the question becomes \"are we improving\", not just \"how are we doing\"."
    - question: "What's in the per-course table?"
      answer: "Every course on the platform with: program type (course, learning path, cohort), parent program when it sits inside one, total enrolled, total completed, average rating, average progress, completion %, engagement %, satisfaction %. Sortable by any column, filterable down to a cohort or program."
    - question: "Can I drill into one course?"
      answer: "Yes. Click into any course from the table and you'll see the individual performance view — the same metrics scoped to that one course, plus a cohort-by-cohort breakdown when the course has multiple cohorts. The detail tells you whether a problem is on the course as a whole or specific to one batch."
    - question: "Can I compare two cohorts of the same course?"
      answer: "Yes. The individual course view lays out every cohort that's run that course with its own row of numbers. Spring vs Autumn, batch 1 vs batch 2, beginner cohort vs advanced cohort — the comparison is a glance, not a project."
    - question: "What about courses that haven't started yet?"
      answer: "They appear in the table with the metrics they have — enrollment count if there's been any, zeros where data hasn't accumulated yet. Once learners start engaging, the numbers fill in. Brand-new courses don't pollute the average; they're weighted by their actual enrollment."
    - question: "Can I export the table?"
      answer: "Yes. Filter the table to whatever slice you want, hit Export, and the CSV lands ready for a board update, a content audit or a quarterly leadership review. The numbers in the file match what's on the screen."
    - question: "Is the data real-time?"
      answer: "Close to real-time. Completion, engagement and satisfaction roll up from the underlying activity within minutes. The twelve-month trend is rebuilt as new data comes in, so the chart you see now reflects the most recent month's read."
    - question: "How is this different from Learner Analytics?"
      answer: "Learner Analytics asks \"how are people doing?\" — engagement, growth, retention across all your learners. Course Performance asks \"how are our courses doing?\" — same data, courses are the unit. Both views are designed to be read together."
    - question: "Who can see Course Performance?"
      answer: "Admins by default, plus instructors with the analytics-view permission scoped to their programs. An instructor sees the courses they teach; a platform admin sees the catalogue. The permission model keeps sensitive numbers in front of the right people."
    - question: "Will it scale on a large catalogue?"
      answer: "Yes. The performance table reads from indexed aggregates so a catalogue with hundreds of courses and tens of thousands of learners stays fast. The headline metrics are pre-computed; the table sorts and filters in milliseconds."
