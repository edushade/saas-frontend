faq:
  title: "Time Spent FAQ"
  description: "The questions that come up the first time you treat time as a real metric."
  items:
    - question: "What does time spent actually measure?"
      answer: "The seconds a learner actively engaged with content. A video tracks watch time — close the tab and the timer stops. A quiz tracks the time between starting and submitting. A text or PDF lesson tracks the time the learner was actively engaged. The total adds up to a number that reflects real attention, not idle browser tabs."
    - question: "Where is time captured?"
      answer: "Everywhere it matters — every lesson type (video, live, audio, text, PDF, embed), every quiz attempt, every assignment session. The platform logs a small delta to a daily learning-time log every time the learner makes meaningful progress on a piece of content."
    - question: "What about a video — does pausing the tab pause the timer?"
      answer: "Yes. The timer follows real engagement, not the time the tab was open. So a learner who walks away with the video paused doesn't accidentally rack up an hour they never watched. When they come back, the timer picks up where it left off."
    - question: "Can I see hours by month, week or day?"
      answer: "All three. The trends view has a granularity toggle — monthly is the default, weekly is good for a Friday check-in, daily is useful during a release week or a campaign. Pick one and the chart and the summary tile both reshape. Up to twenty-four months of history are available."
    - question: "What's the difference between total hours and hours per learner?"
      answer: "Total hours is the platform-wide sum — useful for a headline. Hours per learner is total hours divided by active learners — much more useful for comparing periods, because it accounts for the audience growing. The trends view shows both."
    - question: "Why is the previous-period comparison important?"
      answer: "Because the absolute number rarely tells you anything by itself. \"4,200 total hours this month\" is meaningless without context. \"Average hours per learner up 14% from last month\" is a story. Edushade builds that comparison into every summary, so the take-away is right there."
    - question: "Do learners see their own time?"
      answer: "Yes. Their personal dashboard shows total minutes invested, time per course, and hours this week. They see the same numbers the admin sees of them — useful for self-reflection and for managers in corporate training."
    - question: "Can instructors see cohort-level time?"
      answer: "Yes. The instructor dashboard shows time spent across the cohort and which content is getting the most engagement. Pair that with the question-level analytics on the progress dashboard and you get a strong read on what's working and what isn't."
    - question: "Can I find lessons that are too short or too long?"
      answer: "Yes. Time-spent surfaces the lessons that aren't matching their planned duration. A lesson planned for thirty minutes that averages five tells you something — either learners are skipping past, or the content needs reworking. Either way, you'd want to know."
    - question: "Does time spent feed certificates?"
      answer: "It can. The certificate snapshot stores the total time the learner put into the course at the moment of issuance — useful for accreditation and for learners who want a record of their effort, not just the outcome."
    - question: "Is the data real-time?"
      answer: "Close to it. Time logs flow in continuously as learners engage, with daily aggregates updating throughout the day. The trends view refreshes on a short cadence, so what you see is current to within a few minutes."
    - question: "Is it accurate at scale?"
      answer: "Yes. The time-log layer is built for high volume — every active learner adds time deltas every minute or so, and the aggregates are computed from indexed daily totals, not raw events. The trends view stays fast even on platforms with thousands of learners."
    - question: "How is this different from Learner Analytics?"
      answer: "Learner Analytics tells you who's showing up and how that's changing. Time Spent tells you, of the people who are showing up, how much time they're putting in. Both are aspects of engagement, and the dashboards are designed to be read together — \"are people coming back\" and \"are they leaning in once they do\"."
    - question: "Can I export the data?"
      answer: "Yes. The trends data drops to CSV in one click, with all the granularities (daily, weekly, monthly) available as separate exports. Useful for a board deck, a regulator who wants the source numbers, or piping the data into your own warehouse."
