faq:
  title: "Engagement Insights FAQ"
  description: "What people actually want to know when they sit down with engagement data for the first time."
  items:
    - question: "What does 'engagement' really mean here?"
      answer: "Active participation in the platform — showing up, spending meaningful time, doing things while you're there. The four KPIs (daily active users, session duration, interactions per user, weekly engagement) capture different angles of the same idea: did people actually use the platform, and how deeply?"
    - question: "What's the difference between active users and total users?"
      answer: "Total users is everyone with an account on your platform. Active users is the share who actually showed up in the period you're looking at. The gap between the two is the most honest read on engagement you can get — and the daily-active-users chart traces it across time."
    - question: "How is session duration measured?"
      answer: "It's the time between a learner signing in and either signing out or going inactive. Background tab time doesn't count — the platform tracks active engagement. Average session duration tells you whether visits are quick check-ins or deeper learning sessions."
    - question: "What counts as an interaction?"
      answer: "Anything a learner does that engages with content — opening a lesson, submitting a quiz, leaving a comment, reacting to a post, downloading a file, joining a live class. Interactions per learner gives you the depth metric: not just how many showed up, but how much they actually did while they were there."
    - question: "What's the weekly engagement score?"
      answer: "A blended weekly metric that smooths daily noise into a number you can compare week over week. Useful for board updates and quarterly reviews where day-by-day variation is too granular. The growth percent on it is often the clearest single read on whether the platform is gaining momentum."
    - question: "Why do you compare every metric to the previous period?"
      answer: "Because the absolute number rarely tells you anything by itself. \"3,200 daily active users\" sounds great or worrying depending on whether last month was 2,800 or 3,800. The comparison is built into every tile so the take-away is the trend, not the snapshot."
    - question: "Can I see engagement by user type?"
      answer: "Yes. The breakdown shows learners, instructors and admins with active counts, average session length and percentage of total engagement for each. Sometimes the picture is balanced; sometimes one group is doing the heavy lifting. Either way, the chart makes the situation visible."
    - question: "Can I filter to a single cohort or program?"
      answer: "Yes. The filter row at the top lets you scope to a cohort, a program or a date range. The whole view rescopes — KPI tiles, daily chart, breakdowns. So you can ask \"how engaged is the spring cohort?\" and get an answer without leaving the page."
    - question: "Is the data live?"
      answer: "Close to live. Engagement events update the underlying numbers within a few minutes. Daily charts smooth to the day to keep the read clean, and the weekly engagement score updates as the week progresses."
    - question: "Will engagement always go up?"
      answer: "Honestly, no — and that's part of the value. Engagement dips for real reasons (holidays, exam season, the wrong content at the wrong time) and the dashboard tells you when those reasons hit. The point isn't to chase a green arrow; it's to understand the rhythm of your platform and act on what the chart actually shows."
    - question: "How is this different from Learner Analytics?"
      answer: "Learner Analytics asks \"who's showing up and how is that audience changing?\" — total learners, retention, growth. Engagement Insights asks \"of the people who showed up, how engaged were they?\" — active users, session length, depth. Both views are designed to be read together."
    - question: "Can I export the engagement data?"
      answer: "Yes. The dashboard has a CSV export and individual charts can be downloaded as data. Useful for board decks, campaign post-mortems, or piping the numbers into your own analytics warehouse."
    - question: "Who can see Engagement Insights?"
      answer: "Admins by default, plus instructors with the analytics-view permission. The role-based access keeps engagement data in front of the right people — a teaching assistant might see only their cohort's engagement while a platform admin sees the whole picture."
    - question: "Will this work on a large platform?"
      answer: "Yes. Engagement metrics read from indexed daily aggregates rather than raw events, so dashboards stay fast even on platforms with thousands of learners and millions of events behind them. Filtering by cohort or program keeps even big platforms readable."
