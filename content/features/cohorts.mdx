faq:
  title: "Cohorts FAQ"
  description: "Specifics about how cohorts, schedules, seats, drip rules and instructors work as a batch run of a course or learning path."
  items:
    - question: "What is a cohort in Edushade?"
      answer: "A cohort is a time-bound batch that runs a course or learning path on a schedule. It has its own start and end dates, live class days, seat cap, drip rules, instructor roster and notifications — but the curriculum lives in the bound course or path, so you don't duplicate content per batch."
    - question: "How is a cohort different from a self-paced course?"
      answer: "A self-paced course is evergreen — anyone enrolls any time and progresses on their own schedule. A cohort is a scheduled run with fixed dates, live class days and a max-seats cap, where everyone in the batch moves together. The same course can be offered both ways."
    - question: "Can a cohort run a course or a learning path?"
      answer: "Either. A cohort binds to one programme — a published course or a published learning path — and inherits its curriculum. The cohort then carries the dates, schedule, seats, drip and instructor settings on top."
    - question: "How do live class schedules work?"
      answer: "Pick the days of the week the batch meets, set a start time and an end time, and choose a cadence: weekly, biweekly, monthly, custom or none. Edushade computes the cohort duration in hours from the schedule, and these times surface in cohort listings and notifications."
    - question: "Can I cap how many learners join?"
      answer: "Yes. Set a maximum enrollment cap on the cohort and Edushade tracks seats remaining in real time. Total learner count, days left and remaining seats are computed fields you can display anywhere — in the catalogue, on the cohort page, or in admin reports."
    - question: "How does late enrollment work?"
      answer: "Each cohort has a late-enrollment toggle separate from the enrollment-open toggle. Use them together to keep the door open after the start date, close registrations on a fixed date, or pause enrollments without unpublishing the cohort."
    - question: "Can I run different drip rules per cohort?"
      answer: "Yes. Drip schedules can bind directly to a cohort and target sections, lessons, assignments, quizzes or files. Each cohort can release the same curriculum on a different rhythm — strict weekly unlocks for one batch, accelerated for another — without touching the parent course."
    - question: "Can a cohort have its own instructor roster?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade supports instructor, co-instructor and assistant roles assigned per cohort, in addition to whoever is on the parent course. A featured instructor can be highlighted on the cohort page so learners know who is leading their batch."
    - question: "Are certificates issued when a cohort completes?"
      answer: "Certificates come from the bound course or learning path. When a learner in a cohort clears the passing grade, the certificate issues with their grade, completion date and total time spent — all captured during the cohort run, with a unique number and a public verification code."
    - question: "Can I export cohort data?"
      answer: "Yes. Edushade ships a CSV export of cohort data — title, dates, schedule, seat counts, learner enrollments and metadata — so you can audit a batch, hand it to finance, or move records into another system whenever you need to."
