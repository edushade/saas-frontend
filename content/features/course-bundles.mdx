faq:
  title: "Course Bundles FAQ"
  description: "How bundles work in Edushade — what they group, how pricing applies, status pipeline, and how they differ from learning paths."
  items:
    - question: "What is a bundle in Edushade?"
      answer: "A bundle is a sellable package — its own record on the bundles table, with a title, slug, thumbnail, description, status and pricing — that wraps a set of courses, events and digital products. Buying a bundle grants the buyer access to every entity in the list under the bundle's terms."
    - question: "What can go inside a bundle?"
      answer: "Three resource types from the admin UI: courses, digital products and events. The bundle picker opens to three tabs and lets you select multiple from each, then stores them in a single bundle-entity list keyed by entity type and entity ID."
    - question: "How is a bundle different from a learning path?"
      answer: "A learning path is a curriculum — an ordered sequence of programs a learner walks through with progress tracking, prerequisites and a completion rule. A bundle is a commercial unit — a price-tagged package of items grouped for purchase. The same items can sit in a learning path and a bundle simultaneously."
    - question: "Does each item in the bundle keep its own pricing?"
      answer: "Yes, separately. The bundle has its own pricing options, and so does each course, event or product inside it. A learner can either buy the bundle (one transaction, all items) or any individual item at its own price — both flows live on the same platform."
    - question: "Can I add or remove items after the bundle is published?"
      answer: "Yes. Attach and detach are atomic operations on the bundle-entity list, independent from the bundle's metadata and pricing. The bundle record stays the same; only the contents change."
    - question: "What happens to learners who already bought a bundle when contents change?"
      answer: "They keep what they bought. Purchases are recorded against the bundle and the items in it at purchase time. Adding new items extends what new buyers receive; removing items does not retroactively revoke access from existing learners. The bundle is a moving target for the storefront, not for past purchases."
    - question: "Can I schedule a bundle launch?"
      answer: "Yes. Set the bundle status to scheduled and pick a published_at date and time. The bundle stays out of public listings until that moment, then flips to published automatically. Same flow for seasonal offers, partner promos or batch launches."
    - question: "What pricing options are available on a bundle?"
      answer: "Free, one-time paid and installment pricing — the same engine as courses and events. The pricing record is attached to the bundle's entity ID, so promotions, coupons and offers built on Edushade's billing system apply to bundles without extra configuration."
    - question: "What states can a bundle be in?"
      answer: "Four: draft (in progress, admin-only), scheduled (waiting for a published_at moment), published (live on the storefront), and archived (retired, preserved for history). The record itself is soft-deleted when removed, so audit history is never lost."
    - question: "Can I export bundle data?"
      answer: "Yes. Bundles support a bulk export across filtered lists, so you can pull the full inventory — title, slug, status, dates, entity counts — for backup, finance or integration with another system."
    - question: "Can I bulk-delete bundles?"
      answer: "Yes. The admin supports filter-based bulk delete on the bundles list. Combined with archived state, this means cleaning up an old set of seasonal bundles is one operation, not a row-by-row click-through."
    - question: "Where does the bundle's URL come from?"
      answer: "The slug field. Edushade auto-generates a URL-friendly slug from the title on create — lowercased, hyphenated, ASCII-safe — and you can override it before publishing. The slug becomes the public path, so a bundle reads as a product, not a search result."
    - question: "Can a bundle be reused across cohorts or batches?"
      answer: "The bundle itself is independent of cohorts. The courses inside the bundle can each have their own cohorts, and a learner who bought the bundle can enroll into any cohort the inner courses are running. The bundle is the commercial wrapper; cohorts continue to live on the courses."
    - question: "Are bundles surfaced in the website builder?"
      answer: "Yes. The website builder ships product blocks that pull from the bundle list — so a curated track or an all-access pass appears on landing pages, in feature sections and in catalogue grids the same way individual courses do."
    - question: "Can a bundle contain another bundle?"
      answer: "No. Bundles wrap courses, products and events directly. Nesting bundles is not a supported shape today — if you need a higher-level grouping, run two bundles side-by-side under the same offer or promotion."
